Multiple stressors of ocean ecosystems

Here, we present an interactive interface to explore simulations published in Bopp et al. (2013) to assess how four major stressors of open ocean ecosystems stressors could evolve over the course of the 21st century: sea surface warming, surface pH, subsurface dissolved O2 concentration, and primary productivity by marine phytoplankton (NPP).

Left: Change in stressor intensity in 2090–2099 relative to 1990–1999 under RCP8.5. for the four major stressors averaged over 10 Earth system models. Right: Model-mean time series of the stressors from 1870–2100 using historical and all RCP simulations.
